The First 3 Words You Spot

A word-search built specifically to earn comments, not just likes — the whole point is that people can't answer "which 3 words did you spot first" without typing something. The grid gets reused monthly with new words tied to the month's energy, so the format stays familiar while the content stays fresh.

The caption does double duty: it names the game, sets a soft emotional tone for the month, and closes with a CTA that doesn't feel bolted on. This one pulled 4,077 likes and 3,103 comments — the comment count is the real signal, since that's what the format was built to earn.

A Blue Moon, Explained

A carousel built to do two things at once: correct a common misconception (a "Blue Moon" isn't actually blue) and make a 48-hour window feel worth paying attention to, without tipping into false urgency. "It won't return until 2028" does the job of raising stakes using a real, verifiable fact instead of manufactured pressure.

The caption mirrors that structure — a hook fact, a comment-prompt, then a soft CTA into a personal reading — and it's held up well since posting.

Her Routine vs. Her

An organic-feeling product placement for Nebula Shop's Wealth Fire Candle — split into "her routine" (the objects) and "her" (the person), so the candle sits inside a whole aesthetic instead of standing alone as an ad. No hard sell in the visual; the product just belongs in the scene.

The CTA button does the selling instead of the copy — a single tappable link on the product name, so the Stories set itself stays soft and lifestyle-driven.

May 2026, by Birth Month

A recurring monthly Stories format that sorts a tip for every birth month into one swipeable set — three months per slide, styled like orbiting rings so it reads as a system, not a list. It's a format built to be skimmed: one line per month, no scrolling required to get your own.

The arrow at the bottom does the CTA work instead of text — it just points forward, signaling there's more to swipe to without adding another line of copy to an already-busy frame.
